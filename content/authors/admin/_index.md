Ali Övgün is an Associate Professor of Physics at Eastern Mediterranean University in Gazimağusa, Cyprus. His research lies at the intersection of quantum gravity, cosmology, particle physics, and astrophysics, with a particular focus on probing quantum-gravitational effects through black hole physics and cosmological observations beyond the standard models.

His work centers on understanding and constraining modified gravity theories using black hole properties and cosmological evolution. He has developed and contributed to widely adopted analysis methods for computing Hawking radiation spectra, gravitational lensing observables, and black hole shadow. His broader program spans general-relativistic compact objects, wormhole solutions, stellar structure, and inflationary cosmology — often drawing on the rich landscape of extra-dimensional geometries and importing techniques from mathematics and computational science.

Ali is listed among the World's Top 2% Scientists in the Stanford University/Elsevier rankings (2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024).
